## Purpose

This document defines the conceptual architecture for public-facing meUus institutional expression.

It governs how meUus may publicly represent its reality, purpose, status, boundaries, evidence, commitments, limitations, and corrections without exceeding approved authority or evidence.

The primary public surface considered by this document is `meuus.org`. Other public surfaces may be referenced conceptually, but this document does not create website content, page copy, routes, components, deployment, or implementation.

This is a Working document. It is not Ready for Review, not Frozen, and not canonical.

- public institutional expression.

- public-facing status language.

It does not define public copy, page structure, product content, website implementation, marketing claims, or operational behavior.

### Public Institutional Expression Overview

Public institutional expression means approved public-facing representation of meUus reality, status, purpose, boundaries, commitments, evidence, and limitations.

It is not:

- Foundational Authority.
- Governance Authority.
- Architectural Authority by itself.
- canonical knowledge by itself.
- runtime truth by itself.
- implementation authority.
- marketing permission.

Public institutional expression is a public communication layer constrained by higher authority and evidence. It may explain approved truth, but it does not create truth.

### Public Truth Boundary

Public expression may communicate truth only within the limits of evidence and approved authority.

Public expression cannot:

- create truth.
- create authority.
- exceed evidence.
- silently upgrade status.
- imply future systems are live.
- make private reflection public or canonical.
- replace canonical knowledge sources.
- modify governance or architecture.

The governing rule is:

```text
Claim <= Evidence
```

Every public statement must remain no stronger than the evidence, review state, and authority that support it.

### Public Expression Categories

`meuus.org` may conceptually express the following categories when supported by evidence and approved boundaries:

| Category | Public expression boundary |
| --- | --- |
| Identity | May identify meUus and its public institutional role without overstating capability |
| Purpose | May explain approved institutional purpose without creating new foundational claims |
| Values | May express values inherited from higher authority without redefining Book Zero |
| Current status | May describe current states only within verified evidence and status boundaries |
| Public boundaries | May state what meUus does not claim, does not provide, or has not yet launched |
| Verified documents | May link to or identify Frozen governance and architecture documents |
| Public roadmaps | May communicate Planned or Future direction without implying commitment or operation |
| Non-live systems | May state that a system is Not live when that status is supported |
| Limitations | May disclose uncertainty, missing evidence, or incomplete status |
| Governance links | May point to governance authority without modifying it |
| Correction notices | May correct or narrow public claims when evidence changes |
| Trust and Amanah statements | May express stewardship and restraint without marketing exaggeration |

These categories are permission boundaries, not required copy.

### Prohibited Public Claims

Public surfaces must not claim or imply the following without explicit evidence and required review:

| Claim category | Boundary |
| --- | --- |
| Live platform claims | Requires current operational evidence for the named public capability |
| Validated-assessment claims | Requires validation evidence, scope, limitations, and approved review |
| AI-advisor claims | Must not imply professional, canonical, religious, legal, diagnostic, or crisis authority |
| Therapy, diagnosis, legal, professional, religious, crisis, or emergency support authority | Must not be self-created by meUus and requires applicable external authority and governance |
| Payment, account, backend, or runtime claims | Requires direct evidence for the specific capability |
| Partnership or endorsement claims | Requires discoverable evidence from the relevant external party and approved public expression |
| Impact, safety, performance, or outcome claims | Requires scoped evidence, limitations, freshness, and review |
| Unsupported institutional claims | Must be withheld, narrowed, or escalated |

No public wording may imply these claims merely because a plan, prototype, repository, aspiration, public page, or future architecture exists.

### Public Status Language

Public status labels must follow the taxonomy in `05_evidence_status_and_truth_taxonomy.md`.

| Status label | Public expression constraint |
| --- | --- |
| Existing | May state existence only; does not imply completeness, launch, or operation |
| Verified | Must state what was verified, by what evidence, and within what scope |
| Draft | Must be marked non-authoritative and subject to change |
| Working | Must not be presented as final or canonical |
| Ready for Review | May indicate review candidacy only; approval is not implied |
| Frozen | May indicate approved authority within the document's scope only |
| Planned | Must not imply existence, launch, funding, or commitment |
| Future | Must not imply commitment, availability, or operation |
| Not live | Must not be contradicted by capability language |
| In review | Must not imply approval |
| Deprecated | Must not bypass retirement or Freeze Rule requirements |
| Superseded | Must identify the successor and scope |
| Unknown / Unverified | Must not be softened into confidence |

Public confidence must remain lower than or equal to evidence confidence.

### Public Evidence Requirements

A public claim should identify or inherit the following evidence boundaries:

- source.
- scope.
- freshness.
- limitation.
- status.
- review state.
- public suitability.
- authority relationship.

If any boundary is unknown, the public claim must be narrowed, qualified, withheld, or escalated.

### Public Expression and Governance References

Public pages may reference governance documents, governance status, and architecture releases.

Public pages do not:

- modify governance.
- grant authority to governance documents.
- freeze documents.
- revise the Decision Register.
- reinterpret Frozen documents.
- make architecture operational.

Public popularity, visibility, repetition, analytics, market pressure, user demand, or institutional preference cannot create governance or architecture authority.

### Public Expression and Canonical Knowledge

`meuus.org` may summarize, orient toward, or point to knowledge when the summary is evidence-bounded and faithful to source context.

`meuus.org` does not replace canonical knowledge.

`meuussoul.com` remains the canonical knowledge and learning domain under later governance. Public summaries must not dilute, distort, overclaim, or silently revise canonical sources.

If canonical status, provenance, or review state is unclear, public expression must preserve that uncertainty.

### Public Expression and Runtime Reflection

`meuus.org` may explain the existence, concept, or status of runtime prototypes or future runtime systems only within verified evidence and approved boundaries.

`meuus.org` does not own private reflection data.

`meuus.org` must not imply that `meuus.app` capabilities are more operational, validated, available, private, safe, or authoritative than evidence supports.

`meuus.app` remains the runtime/reflection context under approved limits. Runtime evidence remains evidence, not public truth or authority.

### Public Expression and Future Systems

Future systems must remain clearly Future unless evidence and review justify a different status.

DLAS remains conceptual, Future, Unresolved, and not live unless later Founder-approved architecture defines it.

Public expression must not present a future system as:

- live.
- available.
- validated.
- operational.
- safe.
- complete.
- institutionally endorsed.
- professionally authoritative.

### Review-Gated Public Claims

The following claim categories require review before public expression:

| Claim category | Required review direction |
| --- | --- |
| New institutional claims | Founder Review |
| New capability claims | Evidence review and Architecture Review where architecture/status is affected |
| Status upgrades | Architecture Review or the applicable later review process |
| Partnership or endorsement claims | Founder Review and external evidence |
| Sensitive authority claims | Founder Review and applicable external authority |
| Crisis, professional, religious, legal, therapy, or diagnosis claims | Founder Review and applicable external authority |
| Claims about user data, privacy, or consent | Founder Review and later privacy/consent architecture |
| Claims about DLAS or future runtime systems | Founder Review and Architecture Review |
| Corrections to public mistakes | Review appropriate to the affected claim and authority level |

Escalation pauses stronger publication. It does not approve the claim by itself.

### Public Correction and Retraction Rules

Public mistakes and outdated claims are Amanah obligations, not embarrassment.

When a public claim becomes unsupported, outdated, false, too broad, or unclear:

- outdated claims must be corrected, narrowed, dated, or labeled.
- false claims must be removed or corrected.
- stronger claims require stronger evidence.
- missing evidence must not be hidden.
- conflicting evidence must be acknowledged or escalated.
- status language must be corrected when evidence no longer supports it.

Correction should preserve truth, public trust, provenance, and humility.

### Amanah and Public Trust

Public trust is stewardship, not property.

Public communication must preserve:

- restraint before reach.
- truth before marketing.
- evidence before excitement.
- humility before scale.
- correction before reputation management.
- responsibility before persuasion.

If a public expression would be attractive but unsupported, it must be narrowed or withheld.

### Public Expression States

| State | Meaning |
| --- | --- |
| Expressible | May be stated publicly within evidence and authority boundaries |
| Evidence-supported | Has discoverable evidence sufficient for the bounded claim |
| Review-gated | Requires review before publication or stronger wording |
| Prohibited | Must not be stated under current authority or evidence |
| Future | Possible later expression; not current fact or commitment |
| Not live | Must not be presented as operational |
| Unknown / Unverified | Evidence does not support a confident public claim |
| Correction required | Existing public expression must be narrowed, removed, dated, or corrected |

## Risks

### Public Overclaiming

Risk: public claims could exceed evidence.

Response: require `Claim <= Evidence`, evidence boundaries, and review-gated escalation.

### Authority Confusion

Risk: public pages could be mistaken for governance or architecture authority.

Response: public expression may reference authority but cannot create or modify it.

### Marketing Drift

Risk: institutional explanation could become unsupported marketing.

Response: require truth before marketing and prohibit unsupported impact, safety, performance, availability, or endorsement claims.

### Canonical Knowledge Dilution

Risk: public summaries could replace or distort canonical knowledge.

Response: preserve provenance, limitations, and distinction from canonical sources.

### Runtime Overstatement

Risk: runtime prototypes or future systems could appear live, validated, or authoritative.

Response: preserve status labels, evidence limits, and Future/Not live boundaries.

### Private Reflection Exposure

Risk: private reflection could be moved into public expression.

Response: public expression does not own private reflection data and must escalate privacy/consent claims.

### Outdated Public Claims

Risk: historical public wording could continue implying current truth.

Response: require correction, dating, narrowing, or removal when evidence changes.

### Sensitive Authority Confusion

Risk: public wording could imply therapy, diagnosis, legal, professional, religious, emergency, or crisis authority.

Response: prohibit such claims without Founder Review and applicable external authority.

### Implementation Leakage

Risk: public-expression architecture could become website implementation or copywriting.

Response: keep this document conceptual and exclude UI, routes, components, deployment, page copy, and product features.
