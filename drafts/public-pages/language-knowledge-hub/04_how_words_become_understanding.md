## Simple Explanation

A word is useful, but a word alone is not full understanding. Understanding grows when the learner asks what the word means, how it is used, what context surrounds it, and what responsible action may follow.

## Basic Framework

### Word

The word is the starting point.

### Meaning

The learner asks what the word may mean. Some meanings are approximate and context-dependent.

### Context

The learner asks where, when, and how the word is being used.

### Reflection

The learner asks what the word helps them notice.

### Small Action

The learner chooses one responsible next step.

## Example: Learning

The word "learning" can mean more than receiving information. It may include attention, practice, correction, and patience.

A small action might be: review one mistake and write what changed in your understanding.

## Common Misunderstanding

Knowing a word is not the same as understanding life. Words can help, but they need context, humility, and practice.
