## 2. Purpose

This standard defines how founder data, personal data, contact data, family data, recovery/adversity history, CV/profile details, user-input fields, and public contact routes must be reviewed before public use.

It exists to protect:

- Founder dignity
- Founder privacy
- Family privacy
- User trust
- Personal safety
- Evidence discipline
- Public-safe storytelling
- No overexposure before publication

## 3. Core Rule

NO SENSITIVE FOUNDER, FAMILY, CONTACT, RECOVERY, PRIVATE ARCHIVE, OR USER-DATA-IMPLYING CONTENT MAY BE PUBLISHED WITHOUT DELIBERATE PRIVACY REVIEW AND FOUNDER APPROVAL.

## 5. Public-Safe Handling Levels

| Level | Meaning | Allowed location | Approval needed | Example |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Level 0: Private — do not publish | Material must remain private. | Private archive only | Founder privacy decision; no public approval | Raw private screenshots or full address |
| Level 1: Restricted internal governance only | May be referenced for internal review. | Governance records with care | Founder/governance review | Internal note that a private document exists |
| Level 2: Public-safe summary only | May be summarized without private details. | Draft public copy or reviewed page | Founder review | "Family influence shaped responsibility" |
| Level 3: Public-safe with evidence label | May be public if labeled and bounded. | Public-preview or reviewed public page | Founder approval plus evidence label | "Self-reported learning background" |
| Level 4: Public after explicit Founder approval | May be public in approved wording. | Approved public page | Explicit Founder approval | Public-safe founder profile paragraph |
| Level 5: Public and reusable after separate publication approval | May be reused across public materials. | Approved public pages, profiles, press kit | Separate publication approval | Reviewed founder bio with approved reuse scope |

## 6. Founder Profile Publication Rules

- Do not publish full address unless deliberately approved.
- Do not publish phone number unless deliberately approved.
- Use contact form or protected email policy instead of raw personal exposure where possible.
- Do not publish family details unless deliberately approved.
- Do not publish raw CV without public-safety rewrite.
- Do not publish private recovery/adversity details without dignity review.
- Do not publish private screenshots or payment/gift details unless safely summarized.
- Do not imply credentials, awards, institutional support, or authority without evidence.
- Founder story may be public, but only in evidence-labeled, dignified, bounded form.

## 7. Domain-Specific Privacy Rules

### `meuus.org`

- Public founder identity may appear with public-safe summary only.
- Avoid raw personal contact details unless approved.
- Keep public foundation language separate from private founder archive material.

### `meuussoul.com`

- Remove or guard founder address/contact details unless deliberate approval exists.
- Keep founder/client story public-safe and review-bounded.
- Add non-authority boundaries near personal development/religious/legal content.

### `meuus.app`

- No user data collection implication unless approved.
- Phone/city/profile/input fields must be disabled, mocked, or clearly not-live unless approved.
- Account/profile/payment surfaces need privacy gate before public claim.

### `rujbel.org`

- Founder Living Archive may include founder story, but not raw private archive.
- Lovable preview remains source candidate.
- Public founder archive requires evidence labels and privacy review.
- Private/family/contact/recovery material must be filtered before publication.

## 8. Review Checklist

- What data is being shown?
- Is it founder, family, user, third-party, or institutional data?
- Is it necessary for public trust?
- Can it be summarized instead?
- Is it sensitive?
- Is evidence public or private?
- Does it create safety/contact risk?
- Does it expose family or third parties?
- Does it imply service/user-data collection?
- Does it require disclaimer?
- Has Founder approval been recorded?
- Is publication approval separate?

## 10. Redaction and Rewrite Rules

- Replace full address with city-level or region-level location unless approved.
- Replace phone number with protected contact route unless approved.
- Summarize family influence without naming family unless approved.
- Summarize recovery/adversity without graphic/private detail unless approved.
- Replace raw CV with public-safe profile.
- Remove private screenshots/account/payment details.
- Avoid exact dates or identifiers where unnecessary.
- Never publish third-party private details without consent.

## 11. Approval Record Requirement

Any public use of sensitive founder/contact/family/recovery/user-data-implying content requires a governance approval record before publication.

Approval record must include:

- Content item
- Data category
- Public-safe level
- Evidence label
- Risk level
- Approved wording
- Where it may appear
- Expiry/review date if needed
- Founder approval

## 12. Risk Levels

| Risk level | Definition | Example |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Low | Public-safe identity or already public non-sensitive claim. | Public-safe founder name or broad role statement. |
| Medium | Founder background/story detail needing phrasing care. | Education, work, volunteering, or values summary. |
| High | Address/contact/family/recovery/user-data/payment/profile-field implication. | Founder address, phone, profile input, or recovery detail. |
| Blocker | Private data, unsafe contact exposure, third-party data without consent, or user-data collection without privacy approval. | Raw private screenshot, full address, third-party contact detail, or active user-data form without approval. |
