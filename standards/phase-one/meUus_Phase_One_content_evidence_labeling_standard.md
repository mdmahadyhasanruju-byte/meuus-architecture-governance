## 2. Purpose

This standard defines how meUus content, founder claims, learning material, public pages, domain statements, and future product claims must be labeled according to evidence before public use.

It exists to protect:

- Claim <= Evidence
- Public trust
- Founder privacy
- User safety
- Religious/legal/professional boundaries
- Phase One public truth

## 3. Core Rule

NO CLAIM MAY BE PRESENTED PUBLICLY WITHOUT A CLEAR EVIDENCE STATUS LABEL OR AN APPROVED PUBLIC STATUS CONTEXT.

## 4. Evidence Label Taxonomy

| Label | Definition | Allowed public use | Required caution | Example |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Verified | Claim confirmed by reviewed evidence that can support public use. | May be used publicly with precise wording. | Do not stretch beyond the evidence. | "PR #61 was merged into main" when supported by merge record. |
| Publicly visible | Claim is observable on a public page, route, repository, or public artifact. | May be cited as observed public state. | Visibility does not equal approval or correctness. | "The route is reachable at a public URL." |
| Document-supported but not public | Claim has supporting documents, but evidence should not be exposed publicly. | May be summarized only if approved. | Do not reveal private documents or sensitive details. | "Background detail is document-supported but not public." |
| Self-reported | Claim comes from the Founder or source person without independent verification. | May be used with self-reported label if safe. | Avoid authority, credential, or achievement inflation. | "Self-reported learning history." |
| Founder memory | Claim comes from Founder recollection or origin story. | May be used as personal memory if public-safe. | Must not be treated as institutional proof. | "Founder memory: the domain carries origin meaning." |
| Draft / needs review | Content is incomplete or not reviewed for public use. | Public use only if clearly marked draft and approved for preview. | Do not imply final status. | "Draft public-preview candidate." |
| Public-preview | Content is visible or intended for bounded public preview. | May be public only with preview/status boundaries. | Must not be called final, canonical, or production-ready. | "Language & Knowledge Hub public-preview page." |
| Review-bounded | Content is permitted only within stated review limits. | May be used with visible disclaimer and boundary. | Do not remove or weaken review status. | "Educational only; requires review before public use." |
| Future intention | Claim describes a planned direction, not current fact. | May be used when clearly future-facing. | Do not imply it exists now. | "meUus.app is intended for future guided journeys." |
| Not approved | Claim or action has not passed the required governance gate. | Should generally not be public except as an internal boundary. | Do not present as active, available, or endorsed. | "Indexing not approved." |
| Private / not for publication | Evidence or content must remain internal. | Not allowed for public release. | Protect privacy and confidentiality. | Private founder contact details. |
| Sensitive / restricted | Content may cause privacy, dignity, authority, or safety risk. | Public use only after explicit review and approval. | Use minimum necessary detail and dignity-first wording. | Recovery/adversity or family influence details. |
| Unknown / needs verification | Claim lacks enough evidence or review. | Not for public claim except as unknown. | Must be investigated before publication. | Unverified production status for rujbel.org. |

## 5. Content Status Labels

| Status label | Meaning | Allowed wording | Prohibited wording |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Live | Function or page is currently operational and approved as live. | "Live" only after evidence and approval. | "Live" for future, draft, or unapproved services. |
| Public foundation | Public starting foundation exists, but not full ecosystem completion. | "Public foundation" with not-complete boundary. | "Full launch" or "complete platform." |
| Public-preview | Publicly reachable or viewable as bounded preview. | "Public-preview" / "preview route." | "Final," "official curriculum," "production-ready." |
| Draft | Not final and still under review. | "Draft" / "needs review." | "Verified," "complete," "canonical." |
| Internal only | For governance or private review only. | "Internal only." | Public launch or external announcement language. |
| Governed-incomplete | Audited/planned enough for governance, but incomplete. | "Governed-incomplete." | "Complete," "ready," "production." |
| Future | Intended or planned later. | "Future," "planned," "intended." | "Available now," "live now." |
| Held | Paused pending approval/correction. | "Held pending review." | "Approved," "released." |
| Not production-ready | Not approved for production status. | "Not production-ready." | "Production-ready." |
| Not canonical | Not authoritative or frozen as canonical. | "Not canonical." | "Canonical source." |
| Not approved | No approval exists for the claim/action. | "Not approved." | Any wording implying approval. |

## 6. Authority Boundary Labels

| Boundary label | Where used | Why needed | Example safe wording |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Educational only | Learning pages, Language & Knowledge Hub, topic modules | Prevents service/authority overclaim | "This content is educational only and review-bounded." |
| Reflection only | Journals, prompts, founder notes, self-review material | Prevents diagnosis/advice implication | "This is for reflection only, not professional guidance." |
| Founder story only | rujbel.org, founder archive, origin pages | Separates personal story from institutional proof | "This is Founder story context, not meUus institutional authority." |
| Not legal advice | Legal Hub, law basics, rights/responsibility content | Prevents legal service claim | "This is not legal advice and does not replace a qualified lawyer." |
| Not medical advice | Health, wellbeing, crisis, body/mind references | Prevents medical authority claim | "This is not medical advice or diagnosis." |
| Not psychological diagnosis or therapy | Emotional intelligence, recovery, mental wellbeing content | Prevents therapy/clinical claim | "This is not psychological diagnosis, therapy, or emergency support." |
| Not religious ruling / not fatwa | Quran, Seerah, Islamic values or religious references | Prevents religious authority claim | "This is not a religious ruling or fatwa." |
| Not financial advice | Money, career, business, pricing, investment topics | Prevents financial authority claim | "This is not financial or investment advice." |
| Not employment guarantee | Career, training, skill, certification, journey pages | Prevents outcome guarantee | "This does not guarantee employment or income." |
| Not institutional endorsement | Founder story, partnerships, references, organizations | Prevents implied backing | "No institutional endorsement is claimed unless verified." |
| Not verified curriculum | Language Hub, learning paths, draft modules | Prevents final curriculum claim | "This is not a verified curriculum." |

## 7. Domain-Specific Labeling Requirements

### `meuus.org`

- Use public foundation label.
- Preserve not full ecosystem completion boundary.
- Keep current/future separation visible.
- Do not imply full service launch from public foundation status.

### `meuussoul.com`

- Use Learn/source/reflection language.
- Use public-preview and review-bounded labels where content is still under review.
- Add non-authority labels for Seerah/law/EI/professional topics.
- Do not claim complete curriculum unless separately approved.

### `meuus.app`

- Use future act layer label.
- Use prototype/not-live labels for app/account/AI/DLAS/payment/profile surfaces unless separately approved.
- Do not imply user-data collection unless approved.
- Do not imply live accounts, payments, AI, DLAS, subscriptions, or certification.

### `rujbel.org`

- Use Founder Living Archive label.
- Use governed-incomplete label until production readiness is approved.
- Apply founder story/evidence labels to identity, background, origin, work, values, and archive claims.
- Apply private/sensitive data restrictions to family, address, contact, recovery/adversity, raw CV, and private archive material.
- Treat Lovable preview as source candidate only unless production is verified.

## 8. Claim Review Checklist

- What is the claim?
- What evidence supports it?
- Is evidence public, private, self-reported, or unknown?
- Is the claim current, draft, future, or not approved?
- Does it imply authority?
- Does it involve sensitive founder/user data?
- Does it imply operational service?
- Does it need disclaimer?
- Does it need Founder approval?
- Can it be published safely?

## 9. Public Wording Rules

Allowed:

- "public foundation"
- "public-preview"
- "under review"
- "planned"
- "future"
- "self-reported"
- "Founder story"
- "educational only"

Not allowed unless approved:

- "complete"
- "fully verified"
- "officially launched"
- "production-ready"
- "AI is live"
- "DLAS is live"
- "payments are open"
- "certified"
- "legal advice"
- "religious authority"
- "clinical support"
- "guaranteed outcome"

## 11. Risk Levels

| Risk level | Definition | Example |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Low | Minor wording, consistency, or clarity issue with limited safety impact. | Spelling or minor wording issue. |
| Medium | Ambiguous claim that may imply completion, authority, or stronger status than evidence supports. | Completion/authority ambiguity. |
| High | Claim or surface may imply payment, account, user-data collection, founder privacy exposure, or operational service. | Payment/account/user-data/founder privacy implication. |
| Blocker | Claim is unsafe or materially false under governance boundaries. | False live service, professional authority, sensitive data exposure, unapproved public launch claim. |
