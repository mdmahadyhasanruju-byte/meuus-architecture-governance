## Purpose

This document defines the conceptual architecture for meUus canonical knowledge and provenance.

It explains how knowledge may become canonical, how provenance must travel with knowledge, how source integrity is preserved, and how canonical knowledge remains distinct from public expression, runtime reflection, governance documents, and Foundational Authority.

The primary knowledge surface considered by this document is `meuussoul.com`. Other knowledge surfaces may be referenced conceptually, but this document does not create content for `meuussoul.com`, write book content, write religious content, create learning materials, or define implementation.

This is a Ready for Review document. It is not Frozen and not canonical.

### Canonical Knowledge Overview

Canonical knowledge means approved knowledge material that has:

- provenance.
- review status.
- defined scope.
- stated limitations.
- evidence support.
- authority boundaries.
- source-integrity expectations.

Canonical knowledge is not automatically created by publication, popularity, runtime use, user demand, analytics, public visibility, repetition, or institutional preference.

Canonical knowledge may be preserved, referenced, taught, adapted, summarized, or presented only within its approved scope and provenance boundaries.

### Provenance Overview

Provenance means the traceable origin and custody of knowledge.

Provenance should identify:

- source.
- author or origin.
- date or freshness.
- version.
- transformation history.
- review state.
- scope.
- limitations.
- evidence basis.
- relation to Book Zero, governance, and architecture.
- public-expression suitability.
- runtime-reference suitability.

Missing provenance prevents canonical status.

### Canonical Knowledge and Foundational Authority

Book Zero remains Foundational Authority.

Canonical knowledge does not replace Book Zero, revise Book Zero, or create Foundational Authority. It may inherit constraints from Foundational Authority, but it cannot define foundational human dignity, ultimate truth, theology, ethics, or constitutional assumptions.

If proposed canonical knowledge appears to conflict with Book Zero, the conflict must be escalated to Founder Review.

### Canonical Knowledge and Governance Authority

`00_READ_FIRST.md` remains Governance Authority.

Governance documents define process, lifecycle, review rules, freeze rules, and authority behavior. Canonical knowledge is not governance and cannot amend governance.

Canonical knowledge cannot:

- modify Frozen architecture.
- revise governance lifecycle.
- create Decision Register entries by itself.
- assign authority to itself.
- bypass Founder Review or Architecture Review.

### Canonical Knowledge and Public Expression

`meuus.org` may summarize, orient toward, or point to canonical knowledge when public expression remains faithful to source, scope, status, and limitations.

Public summaries do not replace canonical sources.

Marketing, public visibility, public repetition, or public enthusiasm cannot create canonical status. Public expression must not dilute, distort, strengthen, or silently revise canonical knowledge.

If public expression needs to simplify canonical knowledge, the simplification must preserve source integrity and uncertainty.

### Canonical Knowledge and Runtime Reflection

Runtime reflection may reference approved knowledge only within defined limits.

Runtime adaptation cannot rewrite canonical knowledge. Runtime evidence cannot create canonical knowledge. User reflections are not automatically canonical knowledge.

Runtime frequency, personalization, popularity, analytics, or user demand cannot create canonical status.

If runtime behavior produces candidate evidence or insight, it may be proposed for later review, but it remains evidence or reflection until approved through the proper process.

### meuussoul.com Knowledge Role

`meuussoul.com` is the conceptual knowledge, learning, and canonical preservation surface under approved governance.

It may preserve or present canonical knowledge only within provenance and review boundaries.

This document does not define actual routes, pages, content models, UI, storage, deployment, or implementation for `meuussoul.com`.

### Knowledge Status Labels

Knowledge status labels must remain bounded by evidence and review.

| Status | Meaning |
| --- | --- |
| Draft knowledge | Initial, exploratory, non-canonical knowledge material |
| Working knowledge | Substantially developed knowledge material still subject to revision |
| Review-candidate knowledge | Knowledge material complete enough for formal review but not yet canonical |
| Canonical knowledge | Approved knowledge within a defined scope, provenance, and authority boundary |
| Frozen knowledge | Canonical knowledge locked under a defined change process, if such process is later approved |
| Provisional knowledge | Temporarily usable with explicit uncertainty, scope, and limitations |
| Non-canonical knowledge | Material that may be useful but does not carry canonical status |
| Deprecated knowledge | Material whose use is discouraged or expected to end, without silently erasing history |
| Superseded knowledge | Material replaced by an identified successor within a documented scope |
| Unknown / Unverified knowledge | Material whose source, scope, status, or evidence is insufficient |
| Correction required | Material requiring review, narrowing, correction, or withdrawal |

No status label may be silently upgraded by publication, public use, runtime use, repetition, or demand.

### Provenance Requirements

Each canonical knowledge item should eventually preserve:

- source.
- author or origin.
- date or freshness.
- version.
- transformation history.
- review state.
- scope.
- limitations.
- evidence basis.
- relation to Book Zero.
- relation to governance.
- relation to architecture.
- public-expression suitability.
- runtime-reference suitability.

If a required provenance field is unknown, that uncertainty must travel with the knowledge material.

### Transformation Rules

Summaries, translations, adaptations, excerpts, learning materials, public explanations, and runtime references are transformations of knowledge.

Transformations must preserve:

- source identity.
- scope.
- limitations.
- uncertainty.
- review state.
- transformation history.
- distinction between original source and derived form.

Transformations must not:

- silently upgrade status.
- remove limitations.
- distort meaning.
- become marketing.
- imply authority not reviewed.
- present Future, Unverified, or provisional material as canonical.
- turn public explanation into canonical source.
- turn runtime adaptation into canonical source.

### Special Knowledge Categories

| Category | Boundary |
| --- | --- |
| Book Zero-related material | Must not revise or replace Foundational Authority |
| Founder-originated knowledge | Requires provenance and review before canonical status |
| Books and manuscripts | Require source, version, authorship, status, and publication boundary |
| Learning materials | Must preserve relation to source and not overclaim canonical status |
| Seerah/Quran-related materials | Must not create religious rulings or unsupported religious authority claims |
| DLAS materials | Remain conceptual, Future, Unresolved, and not live until later approved architecture defines them |
| External-source knowledge | Requires source review, provenance, limitation, and authority boundary |
| User-authored reflections | Remain contextual and private unless an approved boundary permits another use |
| Public excerpts | Must preserve source integrity, limitation, and public-expression suitability |

These categories are architectural boundaries, not knowledge content.

### Conflict, Correction, and Deprecation

Conflicting sources trigger review.

Outdated sources must be rechecked before supporting current claims.

Errors in canonical knowledge require correction rather than concealment. Stronger status requires stronger provenance. Missing provenance prevents canonical status.

Correction may require:

- narrowing the claim.
- updating the source relationship.
- adding limitations.
- changing status.
- marking material Deprecated or Superseded.
- escalating to Founder Review or Architecture Review.

Evidence conflicting with Frozen architecture does not silently amend architecture. It may trigger review and, if needed, the Freeze Rule.

### Approval and Escalation

| Matter | Required escalation |
| --- | --- |
| Apparent conflict with Book Zero | Founder Review |
| Change to governance or Frozen architecture | Freeze Rule and required reviews |
| Canonical status criteria | Founder Review and Architecture Review unless later delegated |
| Knowledge status upgrade | Review appropriate to the claim and evidence |
| Public use of canonical knowledge | Public expression boundaries in Document 06 |
| Runtime use of canonical knowledge | Later runtime architecture where needed |
| Seerah/Quran-related authority claims | Founder Review and applicable qualified external review |
| Legal, therapeutic, diagnostic, professional, or crisis-related claims | Founder Review and applicable external authority |
| DLAS knowledge or status claims | Founder Review and later architecture |
| Conflicting or outdated source evidence | Architecture Review where architecture is affected; otherwise later appropriate review |

Escalation does not approve the claim. It pauses stronger status until the appropriate authority resolves or defers it.

### Prohibited Canonical Claims

The following must not be treated as canonical without explicit evidence and review:

- religious authority claims.
- legal, professional, therapy, diagnosis, crisis, or emergency authority claims.
- final-publication claims without publication evidence.
- validated-assessment claims.
- AI-advisor claims.
- impact, safety, performance, or outcome claims.
- partnership or endorsement claims.
- runtime or user reflections treated as canonical.
- public popularity treated as canonical validation.
- founder-originated material treated as canonical without provenance and review.
- external-source material treated as canonical without provenance and source review.

No public surface, runtime surface, or implementation surface may create canonical status by use.

### Amanah and Knowledge Stewardship

Knowledge preservation is stewardship, not ownership inflation.

Canonical knowledge requires:

- truth before influence.
- provenance before presentation.
- humility before teaching.
- correction before reputation.
- source integrity before reach.
- evidence before confidence.

If knowledge cannot be traced, bounded, reviewed, or corrected, it must not be presented as canonical.

### Knowledge States

| State | Meaning |
| --- | --- |
| Draft | Exploratory knowledge material, not canonical |
| Working | Substantially developed but still subject to revision |
| Review-candidate | Complete enough for formal review, not canonical |
| Canonical | Approved within defined scope, provenance, and authority boundaries |
| Frozen | Locked under a defined change process, if later approved |
| Provisional | Usable only with explicit uncertainty, scope, and limitations |
| Non-canonical | Useful or contextual material without canonical status |
| Deprecated | Discouraged or pending removal from use |
| Superseded | Replaced by an identified successor within a documented scope |
| Unknown / Unverified | Insufficient evidence or provenance |
| Correction required | Must be narrowed, corrected, rechecked, or withdrawn |

## Risks

### Foundational Authority Confusion

Risk: canonical knowledge could be confused with Foundational Authority.

Response: Book Zero remains Foundational Authority; canonical knowledge cannot replace it.

### Governance Confusion

Risk: canonical knowledge could be treated as governance or architecture authority.

Response: canonical knowledge is governed by architecture but cannot amend governance or Frozen architecture.

### Provenance Loss

Risk: source, authorship, review state, or transformation history could be lost.

Response: require provenance boundaries and prevent canonical status when provenance is missing.

### Public Dilution

Risk: public summaries could dilute, distort, or replace canonical sources.

Response: public expression may point to or summarize knowledge only within source-integrity limits.

### Runtime Distortion

Risk: runtime adaptation or user reflection could rewrite canonical knowledge.

Response: runtime evidence remains evidence or reflection, not canonical knowledge.

### Marketing Drift

Risk: canonical status could become marketing authority.

Response: prohibit marketing drift and require truth before influence.

### Religious Authority Overclaiming

Risk: Seerah/Quran-related materials could be mishandled or presented as unsupported religious authority.

Response: this document creates no religious rulings and requires Founder Review plus applicable qualified external review where needed.

### Professional Authority Confusion

Risk: knowledge could imply legal, therapeutic, diagnostic, professional, emergency, or crisis authority.

Response: such claims are prohibited without Founder Review and applicable external authority.

### DLAS Overdefinition

Risk: DLAS-related material could make DLAS appear defined, validated, operational, or live.

Response: DLAS remains conceptual, Future, Unresolved, and not live unless later Founder-approved architecture defines it.

### Implementation Leakage

Risk: canonical-knowledge architecture could become content management, product, or platform implementation.

Response: exclude UI, routes, components, databases, APIs, deployment, content creation, and runtime implementation.
