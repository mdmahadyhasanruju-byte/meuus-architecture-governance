## 3. Language Scope

### A. Bangla Language Content

Review needs:
- spelling consistency
- standard vs colloquial Bangla
- Bangla pronunciation spelling
- formal educational tone
- student-friendly phrasing
- technical terms
- Islamic terms in Bangla context

### B. English Language Content

Review needs:
- plain-language clarity
- grammar and consistency
- non-native learner accessibility
- avoid over-advanced wording on beginner pages
- consistent definitions
- example sentence quality
- professional language register

### C. Arabic Language Content

Review needs:
- Arabic spelling
- diacritics where needed
- Modern Standard Arabic vs Quranic/Classical Arabic distinction
- gender/number/case issues
- root/word-family accuracy
- meaning range and context
- Quran/Seerah sensitivity
- no tafsir/fatwa implication

### D. Transliteration Content

Review needs:
- Arabic transliteration in Latin script
- Arabic transliteration in Bangla script
- Bangla pronunciation in Latin script
- consistency across tables
- handling sounds that do not exist cleanly in Bangla or English
- avoiding misleading pronunciation simplification

### E. Trilingual Vocabulary Tables

Review needs:
- word equivalence accuracy
- one-to-one translation risk
- synonym and context differences
- duplicate entries
- inconsistent meaning notes
- word category accuracy
- example sentence accuracy
- source/provenance status for each term

### F. Example Sentences

Review needs:
- naturalness in each language
- grammar correctness
- cultural appropriateness
- religious sensitivity
- age/student suitability
- avoiding legal/medical/financial/religious authority claims

### G. Audio/Pronunciation Content

Review needs:
- native speaker validation
- accent/dialect clarity
- Arabic pronunciation caution
- tajweed boundary where Quranic terms appear
- AI feedback not live unless implemented
- recording quality and consent if voice assets are used

## 6. Translation Policy

- Avoid claiming exact equivalence when words differ by context.
- Use "approximate meaning" where needed.
- For Arabic religious terms, include context labels.
- For Quran/Seerah vocabulary, state "language-learning support only."
- For beginner pages, use simple meanings first, then deeper context later.
- Keep literal meaning separate from applied meaning.
- Keep vocabulary meaning separate from religious/legal/medical advice.
- Avoid using one English word to represent all meanings of an Arabic/Bangla term.
- Maintain version history for corrected vocabulary.

## 7. Transliteration Policy

- Use one standard transliteration system per language layer where possible.
- Explain that transliteration is approximate.
- Arabic sounds may not map perfectly into Bangla or English.
- Bangla script pronunciation for Arabic must be reviewed carefully.
- Do not use transliteration as a replacement for learning original script.
- Mark all transliteration as Draft until reviewed.
- Quranic pronunciation must not be treated as tajweed instruction unless reviewed by qualified teachers.

## 8. Quran/Seerah Language Boundary

- Quran/Seerah vocabulary support is language-learning support only.
- It is not tafsir.
- It is not fatwa.
- It is not fiqh ruling.
- It is not religious authority.
- It should encourage learners to consult qualified scholars/teachers for religious interpretation.
- Any Quranic Arabic content requires religious-boundary review before public use.
- Any Seerah content requires source/provenance review and respectful presentation.
