- unverified public-system claims.
- operational details that could imply service readiness.
- passages that sound like governance amendment.
- passages that sound like therapy/legal/religious authority.
- any claim that future systems are live.

## Safe Excerpt Principles

- Use short excerpts only.
- Preserve context.
- Do not quote sensitive private material publicly.
- Do not convert book language into governance law.
- Do not use poetic language to imply operational readiness.
- Add status labels wherever future systems are mentioned.
- Add disclaimers for legal, therapy, recovery, religious, AI, DLAS, and research-adjacent themes.
- Prefer principle-level excerpts over personal-detail excerpts.
- Prefer truthful-beginning language over emotional proof language.
- Avoid outcome guarantees.
- Avoid capability claims unless evidence and status labels are attached.
- Avoid public excerpts that require the reader to know private founder context.
