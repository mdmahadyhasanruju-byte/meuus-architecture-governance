## 3. Content Scope

### A. Arabic Language Learning

Covers:
- Arabic words
- Arabic pronunciation
- Arabic transliteration
- Arabic in Bangla script
- Arabic grammar basics
- Modern Standard Arabic vs Classical/Quranic Arabic distinction

Boundary:
Arabic language support is not religious ruling.

### B. Quran Vocabulary Support

Covers:
- Quranic vocabulary previews
- word meanings
- pronunciation support
- root/word-family notes if later added
- reflection prompts

Boundary:
Quran vocabulary support is language-learning support only.
It is not tafsir.
It is not fatwa.
It is not fiqh ruling.
It is not religious authority.

### C. Seerah Vocabulary/Support

Covers:
- Seerah terms
- historical names/events
- character lessons
- reflection prompts
- 80-day Seerah journey references

Boundary:
Seerah support requires source/provenance review and respectful presentation.
Historical claims must be sourced.
Reflection is allowed, but verified scholarship must not be implied unless reviewed.

### D. Islamic Reflection Content

Covers:
- faith-related reflection
- character lessons
- spiritual prompts
- Quran/Seerah-linked self-development

Boundary:
Reflection must be clearly separated from ruling, tafsir, or scholarly verdict.

### E. Tajweed/Pronunciation Risk

Covers:
- Arabic pronunciation
- Quranic recitation-related pronunciation
- audio practice
- AI pronunciation feedback

Boundary:
Pronunciation support is not tajweed instruction unless reviewed by qualified teachers.
AI pronunciation feedback is future only unless implemented and reviewed.

## 6. Safe Wording Library

Unsafe:
"Learn the meaning of Quranic words."

Safer:
"Study introductory Quran-related vocabulary for language-learning support. This page does not provide tafsir, fatwa, or religious rulings."

Unsafe:
"This word means exactly..."

Safer:
"This word may be translated approximately as... depending on context."

Unsafe:
"Quranic Arabic pronunciation feedback."

Safer:
"Arabic pronunciation practice is a future feature concept. Quranic recitation and tajweed require qualified teachers."

Unsafe:
"Seerah teaches us..."

Safer:
"This Seerah reflection is a learning prompt based on sourced material. It should not be treated as a scholarly ruling."

Unsafe:
"Islamic psychology for stress."

Safer:
"Faith-sensitive reflection and wellness education. This is not therapy, medical care, fatwa, or religious ruling."

## 7. Source/Provenance Requirements

Before any Quran/Seerah content becomes public, each item must record:

- source title
- author/scholar/source type
- edition or URL if applicable
- translation source if used
- who summarized it
- review status
- religious-boundary reviewer
- date reviewed
- public status label
- whether it is quotation, paraphrase, reflection, or vocabulary note

## 8. Quran/Seerah Content Rules

- Do not publish Quranic verse translations without noting translation source.
- Do not paraphrase hadith/Seerah claims as fact without source.
- Do not use isolated Arabic words to create religious conclusions.
- Do not present founder reflection as scholarly interpretation.
- Do not mix motivational language with religious rulings.
- Do not provide worship/legal rulings.
- Do not answer fatwa-like questions from this module.
- Do not present disputed matters as settled without specialist review.
- Use humble language: "introductory," "learning support," "reflection," "source candidate," "requires review."
