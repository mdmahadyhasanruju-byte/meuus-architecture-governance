## 6. Universal v0.2 Safety Findings

### Status/Authority Safety

v0.2 is materially stronger than v0.1. Every page includes draft/public-preview, review-required, not-published, not-production-ready, not-canonical, not-live, and not-approved-for-deployment language.

### Claim/Evidence Safety

Claim <= Evidence is preserved. The pages make modest learning-support claims only and avoid verified vocabulary, final curriculum, complete path, and outcome guarantee language.

### Runtime/Feature Safety

Runtime safety is strong. Every page includes the advanced-system boundary, and no page implies live AI, DLAS, accounts, dashboards, payments, subscriptions, certification, cloud storage, automated scoring, or automated decisions.

### Human Safety

The pages avoid diagnosis, therapy, emergency support, legal advice, financial advice, religious ruling, recovery support, and professional service language.

### Language/Vocabulary Safety

Vocabulary risk is contained. The drafts avoid vocabulary tables, describe meanings as approximate where relevant, and keep Arabic/Quran/Seerah material in review-needed/future-sensitive territory.

### Copy/Tone Safety

The tone is plain, humble, educational, and non-sales-heavy. The pages use internal draft framing rather than launch or marketing framing.

### Disclaimer Safety

The shared disclaimer is present across all pages and is stronger than v0.1 because it begins with "internal draft public-preview candidate."

### Public Status Consistency

Public status is consistent with current governance: not public, not published, not canonical, not production-ready, not deployed, and not approved for implementation.

### Revision Scope Discipline

v0.2 followed the correction plan. It did not add new page topics, vocabulary tables, Arabic/Quran/Seerah explanations, Legal Hub content, How to Earn content, EQ exercises, AI/DLAS feature content, account features, or payment/certification claims.
