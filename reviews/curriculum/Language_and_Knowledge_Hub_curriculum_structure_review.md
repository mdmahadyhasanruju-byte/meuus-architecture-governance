## 4. Recommended Curriculum Level Model

### Level 00 — Public Preview / Open Learning

Purpose:
Introductory understanding, safe examples, reflection prompts, basic vocabulary, sample lessons.

Allowed:
- simple meanings
- beginner vocabulary
- public reflection
- low-risk quizzes
- basic learning guides
- public disclaimers

Not allowed:
- certification claims
- professional advice
- legal/religious/medical/financial authority
- paid lab claims
- advanced verified claims without review

### Level 01 — Registered Future / Deeper Study

Purpose:
Guided practice, progress tracking, exercises, worksheets, learner profile, deeper lessons.

Allowed only as Future unless account system exists:
- daily learning plans
- saved progress
- writing practice
- translation practice
- exercises
- learner logs

### Level 02 — Advanced Future / Practitioner Lab

Purpose:
Advanced curriculum, case studies, protocols, professional tracks, templates, assessments.

Allowed only as Future unless product/backend/payment/curriculum approval exists:
- full manuals
- practitioner protocols
- advanced projects
- certification pathway
- paid features

### Level 03 — Specialist/Reviewed Track

Purpose:
Reviewed domain-specific material requiring expert input.

Applies to:
- Arabic/Quran/Seerah
- Bangladesh law
- mental health/recovery
- financial/earning/investing
- professional certification
- child/student learning

## 6. Lesson Structure Model

Each lesson should use this structure:

- Lesson title
- Status label
- Level label
- Review status
- Source/provenance status
- Learning objective
- Simple explanation
- Key vocabulary
- Example sentences
- Practice task
- Reflection question
- Small action
- Quiz or self-check
- Related concepts
- Disclaimer where needed
- Next lesson
- Version note

For vocabulary/concept lessons, include:

- English term
- Bangla term
- Arabic term where applicable
- pronunciation/transliteration
- approximate meaning
- deeper context
- example sentence
- source/provenance status
- review status

## 8. Legal Hub Curriculum Handling

Because newly shared material includes Laws of Bangladesh curriculum:

- It should not be treated as direct Language & Knowledge Hub core curriculum.
- It should be treated as adjacent Legal Hub / professional vocabulary source material.
- It may later become a separate Legal Hub curriculum package.
- It may support Professional Language and legal vocabulary lessons.
- It requires official source verification against Bangladesh Code, Gazette, Supreme Court decisions, regulator portals, and updated laws.
- It must carry "Educational Only" and "Not Legal Advice" labels.
- Emergency/citizen guide content must not replace professional legal advice.
- Hotline numbers must be verified before publication.
- Bangladesh law "updated" claims must not be used unless verification date and source are recorded.
